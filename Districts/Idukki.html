Spice Garden of Kerala

IDUKKI WEATHER
Idukki is one of the 14 districts of Kerala state, India, created on 26 January 1972. This beautiful High range district of Kerala is geographically known for its Mountainous Hills and Dense Forests. For the people of Kerala, Idukki is always associated with Power Generation. About 66% of the State’s Power needs come from the Hydroelectric Power Projects in Idukki.
Idukki which lies in the Western Ghats of Kerala is the second largest district in area but has the lowest population density. Idukki has a vast forest reserve area; more than a half of the district is covered by forests. The urban areas are densely populated whereas villages are sparsely populated. Idukki is also known as the spice garden of Kerala.


Top Sights


Eravikulam National Park

Munnar is among the crown jewels of God’s Own Country and has attracted visitors from around the world for centuries due to its natural splendour. Hidden inside, lies the famous Eravikulam National Park, the home and sanctuary of the Nilgiri Tahr. This sacred haven for this exotic animal is a relief in a time when different species are disappearing off the face of the planet.

Mattupetty Dam

Nestled in the greenish valleys of Munnar, the magical hill station Mattupetty offers tourists a fabulous experience and help them feel the calmness of nature. The undulating tea plantation of the hills here and beautiful rolling grasslands, makes Mattupetty a delightful destination, which deserves a visit.

Anamudi

The name ‘Anamudi’ means the elephant’s hair and it is believed that the mountain got the name when some hairs of an elephant were found at the place by the initial visitors.Being one of the topmost peaks in India, Anamudi stands as the prime tourist destination in Kerala and you can view it when travelling towards Munnar from Udumalpet.

Attukad Waterfalls

Attukad captures one’s attention for its endless trails making it an undisputable part of every trekking enthusiast’s bucket list. Imagine rolling hills, a multitude of waterfalls, rare species abound, and a cool breeze that accompanies you the entire way. The visuals alone arrest your eyes for days as everywhere you turn; all you can see are natural masterpieces.
